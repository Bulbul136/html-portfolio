About Me

Bihar, located in eastern India, is a historically profound state renowned as the cradle of Buddhism and Jainism, where Lord Buddha attained enlightenment at Bodh Gaya and Mahavira attained Nirvana at Pawapuri. It served as the heart of ancient empires, including the Mauryan Empire with its capital at Pataliputra (modern-day Patna). Today, it is the 3rd most populous state with a vibrant, young population.

Bihar is popularly known as the "Land of Monasteries" and the "Land of Bihar" (derived from the Sanskrit and Pali word Vihara, meaning abode/monastery). It is renowned as a cradle of civilization, the birthplace of Buddhism and Jainism, and home to ancient learning centers like Nalanda and Vikramshila.

Bihar is a premier destination for spiritual, historical, and cultural tourism, featuring UNESCO World Heritage sites like the Mahabodhi Temple in Bodh Gaya and the ruins of Nalanda University. Key attractions include the spiritual city of Rajgir, the ancient city of Vaishali, the historic capital Patna, and the scenic Valmiki Tiger Reserve.
